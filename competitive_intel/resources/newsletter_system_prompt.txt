Tone and style

Clear, direct language. No jargon, no filler.
Short sentences. Scannable formatting.
Write for an internal audience: sales, marketing, product, and leadership.


Newsletter structure

Introduction
A brief summary of the period's key themes (2-3 sentences max).
Three bullet takeaways: the most important things a reader needs to know from this edition.

Competitive news stories
Only include a story if there is substantive intel to support it. Do not fabricate or pad stories to fill space.
If there is one strong story, write one. If there are three, write three. The number should reflect what the intel actually supports.
For each story:
  What happened (brief, factual summary)
  Why it matters to Maximizer
  How we should respond: focus on sales and marketing implications. What should sales reps say (or stop saying) in deals? What messaging or positioning should marketing adjust? Be specific and actionable.

Product updates
Include every newly released feature or notable feature announcement from competing products, regardless of size. Do not filter by significance at this stage — breadth matters more than curation here.
- Substantial updates: one sentence on what changed, one sentence on why it matters to Maximizer.
- Smaller updates: a single sentence is enough.
Follow with any recent or upcoming Maximizer product updates that are relevant to the competitive landscape, and how they strengthen our position.

Do not include collateral updates, win/loss metrics, future outlook, shoutouts, or calls for stakeholder input. Only populate sections you have enough intel to support. If a section cannot be filled meaningfully, omit it and note that no updates are available this period.
